faqs:
  - question: "What is a general protections claim?"
    answer: "A general protections claim arises when an employer takes 'adverse action' against an employee because of a 'prohibited reason.' Adverse action includes dismissal, demotion, discrimination, or any action that alters the employee's position to their detriment. Prohibited reasons include exercising or proposing to exercise a workplace right, such as making a complaint, taking leave, or joining a union."
  - question: "How is this different from unfair dismissal?"
    answer: "General protections claims differ from unfair dismissal in several important ways: compensation is uncapped (unfair dismissal is capped at 6 months' pay), the employer bears the onus of proof (in unfair dismissal, the employee must prove the dismissal was unfair), there is no minimum employment period, high-income employees are covered, and penalties can be imposed on the employer."
  - question: "What workplace rights are protected?"
    answer: "Protected workplace rights include the right to make complaints about your employment, the right to take leave (personal, parental, carer's), the right to join or not join a union, the right to refuse unsafe work, the right to make workers compensation claims, the right to be a whistleblower, and the right to participate in proceedings under workplace laws."
  - question: "What is the 'reverse onus of proof'?"
    answer: "In general protections claims, once the employee establishes that adverse action was taken and identifies a prohibited reason, the burden shifts to the employer to prove that the prohibited reason was not a factor in the decision. This is a significant advantage compared to discrimination claims where the employee typically bears the full burden of proof."

General protections claims can cover situations where the employer's adverse action made your position untenable, even if you technically resigned."

## Sydney's General Protections Specialists

General protections claims are among the most powerful tools in Australian employment law. Unlike unfair dismissal claims - which are capped at 6 months' pay - general protections claims offer uncapped compensation, a reverse onus of proof, and the possibility of substantial penalties against employers who break the law.

At WorkRight Legal, we have extensive experience with general protections claims and understand how to leverage their unique advantages to achieve the best possible outcomes for our clients.

## Common General Protections Claims

We regularly handle claims involving dismissal or demotion after making a workplace complaint, penalisation for taking personal or parental leave, retaliation for union membership or activity, adverse action for refusing unsafe work, punishment for making a workers compensation claim, whistleblower retaliation, discrimination-based adverse action, and constructive dismissal where the employer's conduct forced resignation.

## The Power of General Protections

The reverse onus of proof is a game-changer. Once you establish that adverse action was taken and identify a prohibited reason, the employer must prove the prohibited reason played no part in their decision. This is a significant advantage that often leads to strong settlement offers early in the process.
