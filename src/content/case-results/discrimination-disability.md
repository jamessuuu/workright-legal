Our client worked in a financial services role and had a diagnosed chronic health condition requiring periodic medical appointments and occasional work-from-home arrangements. Despite providing medical evidence, the employer refused to make reasonable adjustments and placed our client in a restructure that eliminated their role while retaining comparable positions held by other employees.

We filed a disability discrimination complaint with the Australian Human Rights Commission. During conciliation, we presented evidence showing the redundancy was not genuine and that comparable employees without disabilities were retained. The matter resolved with a confidential settlement that compensated our client for lost income, distress, and career disruption.
