Our client, a mid-level public servant, endured persistent bullying from their direct supervisor including public humiliation, exclusion from meetings, unreasonable workload allocation, and threats regarding job security. Internal complaints were dismissed, and no meaningful action was taken by management.

We applied for an anti-bullying order under the Fair Work Act. The Commission heard evidence from our client and multiple witnesses, and was satisfied that repeated unreasonable behaviour had occurred. A stop order was issued requiring the employer to reassign supervisory responsibilities, implement a workplace behaviour policy, and arrange independent mediation.
