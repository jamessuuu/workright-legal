Our client returned from 12 months of parental leave to discover their management role had been redistributed to other staff. They were offered a junior position with reduced responsibilities and no direct reports, despite assurances before leave that their role would be held. When our client raised concerns, they were placed on a performance improvement plan within weeks of returning.

We lodged a general protections application alleging adverse action for exercising a workplace right (parental leave). The matter proceeded to mediation in the Federal Circuit and Family Court, where we presented a strong evidentiary case linking the demotion to our client's leave. The employer agreed to a settlement that included compensation for lost income, a neutral reference, and an extended transition period.
