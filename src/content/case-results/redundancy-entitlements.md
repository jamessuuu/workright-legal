Our client, a senior IT professional with 12 years of continuous service, was informed their role was being made redundant. The employer offered a severance package that fell significantly short of the National Employment Standards minimum, failed to include accrued long service leave, and attempted to impose an unreasonable restraint clause in the deed of release.

We reviewed the proposed terms, identified the shortfall against statutory minimums and our client's enterprise agreement, and entered negotiations with the employer's legal team. The matter resolved with our client receiving their full redundancy entitlements, all accrued leave paid out, and the restraint clause removed from the deed.
