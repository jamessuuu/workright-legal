Our client, a retail store manager with over eight years of service, was terminated after raising workplace health and safety concerns with senior management. The employer claimed performance issues, but had no documented warnings or performance improvement plan.

We lodged an unfair dismissal application within the 21-day deadline and presented evidence that the termination was directly linked to our client's safety complaints. The Fair Work Commission found the dismissal was harsh, unjust, and unreasonable, and ordered reinstatement with full back pay for the period of unemployment.
