## What has changed?

The Fair Work Commission has announced updated thresholds for unfair dismissal claims, effective from 1 July 2026. These changes affect who can lodge a claim and the maximum compensation available.

The high income threshold has been increased to $175,000 (up from $167,500), meaning more employees are now eligible to bring unfair dismissal claims. The maximum compensation cap has also been adjusted accordingly.

## Who does this affect?

If you earn less than $175,000 per year (excluding superannuation), you are eligible to lodge an unfair dismissal claim with the Fair Work Commission, provided you meet the other eligibility requirements:

- You must have completed the minimum employment period (6 months for businesses with 15 or more employees, or 12 months for small businesses)
- You must have been dismissed, or forced to resign (constructive dismissal)
- The dismissal must have been harsh, unjust, or unreasonable

## The 21-day deadline remains critical

The strict 21-day filing deadline has not changed. You must lodge your unfair dismissal application within 21 calendar days from the date your dismissal takes effect. Late applications are rarely accepted.
